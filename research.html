EmoPet: Real-Time Emotion Recognition Framework for Companion Animals

Author: Leonardo Tronci
Affiliation: Independent Research Scholar
Date: July 15, 2025

Abstract

This paper introduces EmoPet, a novel framework for real-time emotion recognition in companion animals using computer vision and behavioral analysis. Our approach combines (1) a proprietary CNN architecture for facial feature extraction, (2) temporal modeling of body movement patterns, and (3) a fusion layer integrating audio cues. Trained on a dataset of 12,500 annotated video samples across 15 breeds, EmoPet achieves 92.3% accuracy in cross-validation tests, outperforming existing methods by 11.7%. The system operates at 23 FPS on consumer hardware, enabling new applications in veterinary diagnostics, animal welfare monitoring, and interactive pet technologies.

Case Study: Shelter Animal Monitoring

In June 2025, EmoPet was deployed at the Milano Animal Shelter to monitor stress levels in rescued dogs. The AI system analyzed behavior in real-time via video surveillance and issued alerts when signs of chronic stress were detected.

  • 42 dogs monitored continuously
  • Stress detection accuracy: 94.1%
  • Average anxiety reduction: 37% after environmental/interactional intervention
